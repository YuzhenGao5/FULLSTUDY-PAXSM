## 2.01 Principal questions, hypotheses, and objectives

### Amendment Summary

The objectives have been expanded to include formative requirements, task-linked workload evidence, held-out validation, and researcher review while retaining the originally approved PAXSM aims.

### Final Answer / Selection

This research aims to test a new way of answering questionnaires in an Extended Reality (XR) environment and investigate whether recording how people interact with the system can reveal useful information about their mental workload and attention during responding. We call this system the Process-Awareness XR Questionnaire System (PAXSM). It allows users to answer questions through a visual knob-based interaction and records the interaction trace.

The amended project addresses four related questions. First, can the knob-based interaction measure workload and provide acceptable usability compared with a traditional XR panel? Second, which interaction signals, such as response time, initiation latency, re-selections, pauses, reversals, and movement speed, differ between Careful and Speeded/Constrained responding? Third, can task parameters and observable task performance provide useful context for interpreting NASA-TLX Mental, Temporal, and Physical Demand ratings? Fourth, do structured, de-identified records that combine the final rating with calibrated task-side and response-process evidence help XR/HCI researchers review questionnaire responses?

The original mental-arithmetic and questionnaire studies are retained. A new colour-and-shape target-selection task provides Baseline, Mental, Temporal, Physical, and Combined profiles. The Combined data are held out and are not used to calibrate the interpretation rules. A formative study identifies researcher requirements before the participant studies, and a later researcher study evaluates the review records. The system does not automatically declare a response valid, invalid, or careless; the final decision remains with the researcher.

## 2.02 Background and justification

### Amendment Summary

The background has been updated to justify task-side evidence, cross-loading assessment, uncertainty reporting, and researcher-centred review of XR questionnaire records.

### Final Answer / Selection

Answering questionnaires with a knob is an embodied measurement approach: participants answer through a simple body action rather than pointing and clicking flat buttons. This may reduce the abstraction of questionnaire interaction and provide a richer record of how an answer was formed. Most XR questionnaires still reuse 2D click or slider interactions, and prior work has mainly focused on visual presentation rather than whether the answering modality can preserve immersion and expose useful process information.

A persistent challenge in questionnaire research is that a final rating alone provides limited information about the response process or the task episode that produced it. Existing careless-response detectors are generally designed for long scales and are difficult to apply to short in-situ XR questionnaires. In addition, a high workload rating may be difficult to interpret without information about the task parameters, performance, or data quality.

We address these gaps through four linked stages. A formative study identifies what evidence XR/HCI researchers need when reviewing subjective records. The retained PAXSM studies examine feasibility, usability, and Careful/Speeded response traces. A target-selection study tests candidate task profiles linked to Mental, Temporal, and Physical Demand while retaining all six NASA-TLX ratings so that cross-loading can be examined. Finally, researchers review de-identified held-out records to assess whether structured evidence supports review decisions. This staged design evaluates evidence cautiously: candidate indicators may be retained, classified as multidimensional or context-only, or rejected if they do not show a stable and interpretable relationship.

## 2.03 Contribution to new knowledge

### Amendment Summary

The contribution has been updated to include dimension-linked task context and structured researcher-review records in addition to the originally approved feasibility and response-process contributions.

### Final Answer / Selection

1. Extending embodied measurement to XR subjective measurement. The study evaluates whether a knob-based questionnaire remains feasible and usable in an immersive environment and compares it with a traditional panel-based implementation.

2. Item-level response-process evidence for short scales. PAXSM records response time, initiation latency, adjustments, pauses, reversals, movement speed, and other interaction signals. The study examines how these signals differ under Careful and Speeded/Constrained instructions and how they can be presented without automatically labelling a participant or response.

3. Task-linked context for subjective workload ratings. The target-selection study combines NASA-TLX responses with known task parameters and observable performance. This allows candidate indicators for Mental, Temporal, and Physical Demand to be assessed together with cross-loading and uncertainty.

4. Researcher-centred review records. The project develops and evaluates a structured record that binds a questionnaire response to its task episode, response process, provenance, missing-data information, and uncertainty. A formative study informs the design, and a blinded researcher review examines whether the record supports more informed and appropriately confident decisions.

Together, these contributions provide a cautious and reusable workflow for collecting and reviewing interpretable XR questionnaire records. The resulting software and reporting approach are intended to support future XR/HCI research without replacing researcher judgement.

## 2.04 Study design

### Amendment Summary

The study design has been amended from the originally approved two-experiment, 52-participant, one-session design to four linked tasks involving 24 general participants across two sessions, 12 formative researchers, and 12 separate researcher reviewers. A three-profile target-selection study and a non-calibrating held-out Combined block have been added.

### Final Answer / Selection

This amended study consists of four linked tasks and three participant groups, with a total of 48 unique participants. Twenty-four general adult participants complete Tasks 1 and 2. Twelve XR/HCI researchers complete Task 0, and a separate group of 12 XR/HCI researchers completes Task 3. Task 0 and Task 3 researchers will not overlap. All study activities take place at the Human Computer Interaction Lab (Room 303-521), Faculty of Science, University of Auckland, City Campus.

Task 0: Formative Researcher Study

Task 0 recruits 12 researchers who have experience conducting XR/HCI user studies or analysing subjective questionnaire data. In an approximately 45-minute session, they complete a short formative survey, describe their current questionnaire-data review practices, inspect synthetic examples of traditional questionnaire records and an early structured-evidence prototype, and provide written comments about useful evidence, provenance, uncertainty, and researcher control. No real participant records are shown. No audio or video is recorded; the researcher records de-identified written notes.

Task 1: Retained PAXSM Feasibility and Response-Process Study

The same 24 general participants later used in Task 2 first complete the two originally approved experiments in one approximately 75-minute session. The first experiment retains the 2 x 2 within-participant design: questionnaire system (traditional panel-based versus PAXSM) by mental-arithmetic difficulty (simple versus moderate). Each participant completes all four combinations. System and difficulty order are counterbalanced. After each arithmetic task, participants complete the full six-dimension NASA-TLX using the assigned questionnaire system. The SUS is administered once after participants first use each system.

The second retained experiment uses PAXSM to complete the UEQ-S under Careful and Speeded/Constrained instructions. Condition order is counterbalanced. These data provide independent response-process calibration and a normal Careful-response reference. The approved Polar H10, Meta Quest Pro eye tracking, Unity interaction logs, and brief written-note interview procedures are retained for Task 1.

Task 2: Dimension-Linked Target-Selection Study

The same 24 participants attend a second session of approximately 60 minutes. They use the Meta Quest Pro and controller to select objects defined by colour and shape. No Polar H10 or additional physiological data are collected in Task 2. A brief low-workload Baseline block is followed by three candidate profiles: Mental Demand (greater rule complexity, memory, or distractor requirements), Temporal Demand (shorter response windows or externally paced selections), and Physical Demand (greater reaching distance, movement, or target-selection effort). The three candidate profiles are counterbalanced; Baseline remains first for familiarisation and reference.

After each block, participants complete the full six-dimension NASA-TLX through PAXSM. After each NASA-TLX rating, they report their confidence on a five-point scale. The system records task parameters, accuracy, completion time, errors, timeouts, selected targets, corrections, and questionnaire interaction traces. All Baseline and candidate-profile questionnaire responses use a Careful instruction.

The final Combined block contains a mixture of the task characteristics and is identical for all participants. Participants are randomly allocated to one questionnaire-response condition: 12 Careful and 12 Speeded/Constrained. The instruction changes how the post-task questionnaire is answered, not the Combined task content. Combined data are held out: they are stored separately and are not used to select features, set thresholds, tune task-side indicators, or alter wording. They are accessed only after the response-process and task-side interpretation rules have been documented and frozen.

Task 3: Researcher Record-Review Study

A separate group of 12 XR/HCI researchers completes an approximately 45-60-minute blinded review study. Each reviewer views de-identified Combined records under two conditions: a Traditional Record containing the final NASA-TLX response and basic task outcomes, and a CARE-XR record containing the same information plus calibrated task-side and response-process evidence, provenance, missing-data information, and uncertainty. Record assignment and presentation order are counterbalanced, and the same reviewer does not see the same underlying record twice. Reviewers are not shown the Careful or Speeded/Constrained label. They indicate whether further review is needed, the likely source of concern, an appropriate action, their confidence, evidence used, and a short rationale. Review time is recorded.

## 2.06 Main ethical aspects

### Amendment Summary

The ethical discussion has been updated for three participant groups, two general-participant sessions, repetitive target-selection movement, mild time pressure, reviewer blinding, de-identified record display, held-out-data controls, and updated withdrawal and storage arrangements.

### Final Answer / Selection

This amended study involves minimal-risk participation in controlled XR and researcher-review environments. The main ethical considerations are informed consent across three participant groups, data privacy and security, XR and repetitive-movement discomfort, mild time-pressure effects, researcher blinding, right to withdraw, and controlled use of de-identified participant records.

Informed Consent: Separate Participant Information Sheets and Consent Forms will be used for general participants, Task 0 researchers, and Task 3 researcher reviewers. Before any study procedure begins, participants receive a clear written and verbal explanation of the purpose, procedures, duration, data collected, reimbursement, and data handling. They may ask questions and provide written consent. Participation is voluntary, and participants may stop or withdraw without penalty or giving a reason.

Privacy and Confidentiality: All study data are coded using participant IDs. The temporary linkage list is encrypted, password-protected, stored separately on the University of Auckland Research Drive, and accessible only to the Principal Investigator. Task 3 reviewers see only selected de-identified Combined records through the review interface. They cannot access source files, identity links, raw physiological data, or identifying information. Results are reported only in de-identified or aggregated form.

Participant Wellbeing and Safety: XR tasks are completed while seated. Short breaks are provided between blocks, and additional breaks are available on request. Participants may remove the headset or stop immediately if they experience dizziness, nausea, visual discomfort, arm or shoulder fatigue, frustration, or distress. The Speeded/Constrained instruction may create mild time pressure; participants are told that it is an experimental response instruction and not an evaluation of their ability.

Researcher Review: Task 3 researchers are informed that condition labels are withheld for methodological blinding. This is not deception about the purpose or nature of participation. Reviewers assess de-identified records and are not asked to diagnose or make decisions about identifiable individuals.

Held-Out Data: Combined data are stored separately and are not accessed for calibration. The interpretation rules are documented and frozen before the data are released for record generation. This reduces the risk of presenting over-fitted or misleading evidence to reviewers.

Right to Withdraw and Debriefing: Participants may request removal of their data until two weeks after their final session, before the coding list is deleted and data are aggregated. A short debrief is provided after participation, with contact details for questions or support.

Data Management: Electronic data are written to a secure research computer and transferred to the University Research Drive. No audio, video, facial image, or voice recording is collected. Raw physiological and behavioural files remain within the authorised research team. Task 3 reviewers receive only restricted de-identified extracts. Data are retained for six years and then securely destroyed.

## 5.01 Summary of what participants will be asked to do

### Amendment Summary

The participant procedure has been replaced with complete procedures for Task 0 formative researchers, the retained Task 1 studies, the new Task 2 target-selection session, and Task 3 researcher reviewers, including durations and data collected.

### Final Answer / Selection

The amended project contains three participant groups.

Task 0 Researcher Participants (12 participants; approximately 45 minutes)

Participants receive the Task 0 Participant Information Sheet, ask questions, and provide written consent. They complete a short survey about how they currently collect, inspect, and review XR questionnaire data. They then inspect synthetic examples of a traditional questionnaire record and an early CARE-XR evidence prototype. They comment on what evidence is useful, what information is missing, how provenance and uncertainty should be shown, and which decisions must remain under researcher control. No real participant data are displayed. Responses are entered electronically, and the researcher may take de-identified written notes. No audio or video is recorded.

Task 1 and Task 2 General Participants (24 participants; two sessions)

Session 1, Task 1 (approximately 75 minutes): Participants read the Participant Information Sheet and provide written consent before any study procedure. With researcher assistance, they put on the Meta Quest Pro headset and Polar H10 chest strap. The researcher adjusts the equipment, calibrates the eye-tracking function, and teaches the participant how to use the traditional panel and PAXSM visual knob.

Participants complete four short mental-arithmetic tasks representing the approved combinations of traditional/PAXSM questionnaire system and simple/moderate difficulty. After each arithmetic task, they complete the full NASA-TLX. After first using each questionnaire system, they complete the SUS. They then use PAXSM to complete the UEQ-S twice, once following a Careful instruction and once following a Speeded/Constrained instruction; order is counterbalanced. Unity interaction traces, approved eye-tracking and Polar H10 signals, and questionnaire responses are recorded as described in the original approval. A short interview follows, and the researcher takes written notes only. Breaks are offered between tasks.

Session 2, Task 2 (approximately 60 minutes): Participants return to the same lab and use the Meta Quest Pro and controller while seated. No Polar H10 or additional physiological data are collected. They complete a colour-and-shape target-selection task. A brief Baseline block is followed by Mental, Temporal, and Physical candidate profiles; the three candidate profiles are counterbalanced. Participants select targets according to the rule displayed in XR. The task records accuracy, completion time, errors, timeouts, selected targets, corrections, and task parameters.

After each block, participants complete the full six-dimension NASA-TLX using PAXSM. After every NASA-TLX rating, they report confidence on a five-point scale. Baseline and the three candidate profiles use a Careful response instruction.

The last block is Combined. All participants receive the same task content and parameters. Participants are randomly allocated to complete the post-task NASA-TLX and confidence responses under either a Careful instruction (12 participants) or a Speeded/Constrained instruction (12 participants). The Combined data are held out and are not used to calibrate the system. Participants are offered a break between blocks and may stop at any time.

Task 3 Researcher Review Participants (12 participants; approximately 45-60 minutes)

Participants receive the Task 3 Participant Information Sheet, ask questions, and provide written consent. They receive a brief explanation of the review task and then inspect approximately 12 de-identified Combined records. Records are presented as either a Traditional Record or a CARE-XR record, with order and assignment counterbalanced. The same underlying record is not shown twice to the same reviewer. The Careful/Speeded condition is hidden.

For each record, reviewers indicate whether further review is needed, the likely source of concern, an appropriate action, their confidence, which evidence they used, and a short rationale. Review time is recorded. Reviewers cannot access participant identities, source files, raw physiological signals, or the link between participant names and codes. A debrief follows completion.

## 9.08 Risk provisions

### Amendment Summary

The risk-management response has been updated for the second XR session, repetitive target selection, mild time pressure, arm or shoulder fatigue, and the minimal risks of formative and de-identified researcher-review activities.

### Final Answer / Selection

Some general participants may experience temporary dizziness, nausea, visual discomfort, headache, or disorientation associated with XR. Task 2 may also cause mild arm or shoulder fatigue from repeated target selection, and the Temporal or Speeded/Constrained conditions may cause mild time pressure, frustration, or tiredness. All XR tasks are completed while seated. The researcher remains present, offers short breaks between blocks, and provides additional breaks on request. Task parameters will be piloted for comfort, and the task will not require painful, forceful, or extreme movements.

Participants may remove the headset, decline a task, pause, or stop immediately without penalty. If dizziness or another adverse effect occurs, the procedure will be paused or terminated. The participant will remain seated and be given time and water until they feel stable. If symptoms persist, they will be advised not to drive or operate machinery and to seek medical advice if needed. The Speeded/Constrained instruction is explained as an experimental instruction rather than an assessment of ability.

Task 0 and Task 3 involve surveys and review of synthetic or de-identified research records and are expected to present minimal risk. Researcher participants are not asked to evaluate identifiable colleagues or make employment, academic, or clinical decisions. They may skip any open-text question or stop the session.

No audio, video, or facial-image recording is collected. Any adverse event or near miss will be documented, reported to the supervisor, and reported to the ethics committee where required. Relevant University support contacts will be included in each Participant Information Sheet.

### Final Answer / Selection

This amendment retains the originally approved studies while extending the project to examine how task-side and response-process evidence can support the interpretation and review of XR questionnaire records. Following refinement after the application was unlocked, the requested scope has been narrowed: the amendment does not include six separate NASA-TLX dimension tasks and does not include the locally running chat-based assistant described in the unlock correspondence.

The amendment makes the following changes. First, the number of general participants is reduced from 52 to 24, and the same 24 participants attend two sessions. It contains a brief Baseline block, three candidate workload profiles linked to Mental, Temporal, and Physical Demand, and a Combined held-out block. After each block, participants complete the full six-dimension NASA-TLX and report confidence in each rating. Task parameters, accuracy, completion time, errors, timeouts, selections, and questionnaire interaction traces are recorded.

The Combined block is not used to calibrate or tune any interpretation rule. Participants are randomly allocated to a Careful or Speeded/Constrained questionnaire-response instruction (12 per condition). Combined data are stored separately and are not accessed until the response-process and task-side rules have been documented and frozen.

Third, Task 0 recruits 12 XR/HCI researchers for a formative survey and prototype review. Fourth, Task 3 recruits a separate group of 12 XR/HCI researchers to review de-identified Combined records under Traditional and CARE-XR record conditions. The reviewer study records review decisions, evidence use, confidence, rationale, and review time. The total intended sample is 48 unique participants: 24 general participants, 12 formative researchers, and 12 researcher reviewers.

Separate recruitment materials, Participant Information Sheets, Consent Forms, study instruments, and question lists will be supplied for the general-participant, formative-researcher, and researcher-review groups.
